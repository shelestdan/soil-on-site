When Do You Need an OSSM Report?

You will typically require a formal assessment when:

  • Proposing a new dwelling or subdivision in unsewered areas.
  • Installing, replacing, or upgrading an existing septic system.
  • Council requests a report to support a Development Application (DA).
  • Adding bedrooms, a secondary dwelling, guest house, or another building that changes wastewater load.

System types we assess

Our reports provide specific guidance on the best system for your land, including Septic Systems, AWTS, Greywater Systems, Composting Systems, and Biological Filters.

During the fieldwork we drill boreholes to 1.5m/2m depth on the proposed effluent disposal area. Soil testing includes:

  • Visual soil description
  • Soil texture and structure
  • pH
  • Electrical conductivity
  • Dispersity

It is common for councils to issue a "Request for Further Information" (RFI) during the DA process. Wastewater designs are legally tied to the specific "load" (number of bedrooms) and "setbacks" (distances from buildings) of your site plan. If you change the number of bedrooms or the footprint of the house after the report is issued, the assessment must be updated to remain compliant with AS/NZS 1547:2012.

Please note that additional fees apply for report updates to cover the technical recalculation and drafting time required.
